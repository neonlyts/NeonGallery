# 📸 NeonGallery — An AI-Built Photography Portfolio

> **An experiment:** Can an AI build a complete, polished website from just prompts?  
> Spoiler: pretty much, yes.

🔗 **Live Site:** [neon-photo-studio--neonlyts.replit.app](https://neon-photo-studio--neonlyts.replit.app/)

---

## 🧪 What This Is

This isn't a project where I wrote the code — that's the point.

NeonGallery is a photography portfolio website built entirely through **Replit AI**, driven by prompts. The goal was to explore how capable AI-assisted development has become, and whether it could produce something genuinely usable and visually polished without manual coding.

---

## 🤖 How It Was Built

| Step | What happened |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | Prompted Replit AI to create a dark-themed photography portfolio |
| 2 | Chose a cover photo to set the visual tone |
| 3 | Replit AI followed up with next-step suggestions — not just executing, but guiding |
| 4 | AI proposed and implemented entrance animations for landing page photos |
| 5 | Iterated through prompts until the result felt right |

No templates were picked from a library. No code was manually written or edited.

---

## 📷 The Photos

All photos on the site are from my **own photography** — a personal hobby.  
No stock images. No placeholders. Everything you see was shot by me. This can be validated from my original photography profile if required - https://500px.com/p/neonlyts

This was important to the experiment: the AI had to work with *real, curated content*, not filler.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Replit AI didn't just generate code — it **collaborated**, asking clarifying questions and suggesting features I hadn't thought of
- Dark theme, layout, animations, and responsiveness were all handled through conversation
- The gap between "prompt" and "deployable website" is surprisingly small now
- AI tools shine brightest when you bring your own content and a clear vision
